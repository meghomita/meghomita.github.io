**About Me** (pronouns: she/they) <br>
![an image alt text]({{ site.baseurl }}/images/das_meghomita.jpg#left) 
I completed my PhD thesis in the Department of Earth & Planetary Sciences, at McGill University. _[My thesis](https://escholarship.mcgill.ca/concern/theses/zg64ts442)_ explored the structural heterogeneity within the Franciscan Subduction Complex in Angel Island, California in order to characterize the behaviors of rocks responsible for producing slow slip and tremor at the deep end of the subduction interface. Recently, I have transitioned to the mineral exploration industry wherein I bring my structural geology expertise to understanding geometry of mineral deposits in tectonically complex terranes. With my multi-disciplinary expertise, I am open to consultancy-based roles within the domains of structural geology, rock mechanics and seismic hazard risk assessments. <br>

Besides being a geoscientist, I am also interested in science communication and science policy-based projects. I have volunteered with the Science and Policy Exchange, a student-led not profit organization that works with students and leaders across industry, academia, and government to champion for science policy within the Canadian political landscape. I have dabbled in multiple science communication projects at McGill University on the themes of public outreach of geosciences, decolonozing and diversifying STEM, documeting relevance of science communication and implementing various initiatives tailored towards fieldwork safety protocols and increasing belonging in STEM. As a science writer for Temblor Earthquake News, I have covered several stories on the themes of geohazards and earthquakes. At present, I am available to collaborate on potential science communication and geoscience outreach based projects. <br>
